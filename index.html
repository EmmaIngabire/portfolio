Subtheme: Demographic and Geographic Analysis of Fatal Accidental Overdoses in Allegheny County

Introduction

In this assignment, we will perform an exploratory data analysis (EDA) on a dataset from the Western Pennsylvania Regional Data Center, which documents fatal accidental overdoses in Allegheny County. This dataset includes detailed information on overdose incidents, such as age, gender, race, drugs involved, and the zip codes of both the incident and residence. The goal of this analysis is to uncover trends and patterns in overdose deaths, providing insights into the demographic and geographical factors associated with these incidents. By examining the data, we aim to better understand the dynamics of the overdose crisis in the county and identify potential areas for intervention.

Dataset:Fatal Accidental Overdoses in Allegheny County

The dataset, titled "Fatal Accidental Overdoses in Allegheny County," is published monthly by the Western Pennsylvania Regional Data Center and contains information on fatal accidental overdoses occurring in Allegheny County. It includes variables such as Age, Gender, Race, Drugs Present, Zip Code of Incident, and Zip Code of Residence.
This dataset is available for download in CSV format from the following link: Fatal Accidental Overdoses Dataset.

Discoveries & Insights

Summary

The report provides a comprehensive analysis of fatal accidental overdose deaths, revealing several key insights. Age distribution analysis indicates that individuals aged 30 to 35 experience the highest number of overdose deaths, contrary to the initial expectation of a younger age group being more affected. Gender-wise, males are significantly more affected than females, with 4,934 male deaths compared to 2,202 female deaths, supporting the notion that drug use is more prevalent among men. Racial disparities are evident, with White individuals having the highest death toll at 5,679, followed by Black individuals at 1,377, suggesting a pronounced racial gap in overdose fatalities. The analysis of drug types highlights that Fentanyl, Cocaine, and Heroin are the leading causes of overdose deaths. Geographic analysis of overdose deaths across various areas of Pittsburgh reveals that certain neighborhoods experience higher rates of fatalities, which could inform targeted intervention strategies. Additionally, trends over the years show fluctuations in overdose death rates, with the highest numbers recorded in 2017, 2016, 2022, 2021, and 2023, indicating variability in the impact of overdoses over time.
